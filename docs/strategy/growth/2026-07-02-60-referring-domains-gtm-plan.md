# VisuTry 60 Referring Domains GTM Plan

Date: 2026-07-02
Status: Active source of truth
Owner: Codex, with founder support where identity, login, 2FA, or outbound approval is required
Primary product: Free Face Shape Detector
Primary URL: `https://www.visutry.com/en/face-shape-detector`

## 1. Outcome

Build 60 verified links from 60 independent referring domains to VisuTry.

This is an acquisition outcome, not an activity target. Submissions, emails, replies, and pending reviews do not count toward 60 until a live link is verified.

The campaign uses the free, private, on-device Face Shape Detector as the strongest acquisition offer and connects qualified visitors to Glasses Advisor, Virtual Try-On, and Frame Compare.

## 2. Acceptance standard

A placement counts when all of the following are true:

1. It is on a public page on a unique registrable domain.
2. The page contains a clickable link to `visutry.com`.
3. The destination resolves to a valid VisuTry page.
4. The linking page is indexable or belongs to a legitimate moderated platform that search engines can crawl.
5. The site is topically relevant, a legitimate product/tool directory, a real publication, a partner, or a genuine founder community.
6. The live URL, anchor text, target URL, link attribute, discovery date, and verification date are recorded.

The following do not count:

- multiple links from the same registrable domain;
- VisuTry-owned domains or subdomains;
- X, Reddit, Facebook, or other transient social posts;
- URL shorteners, scraper mirrors, copied search pages, comment spam, PBNs, or hacked pages;
- a submission confirmation without a live public page;
- paid links that hide sponsorship or exist only to manipulate ranking.

`nofollow`, `ugc`, or `sponsored` links may count as legitimate referring domains, but their attributes must be recorded. At least 30 of the 60 should be editorial or standard directory links rather than user-generated community links.

## 4. Channel portfolio

The 60-link goal is deliberately spread across channels so failure in one channel does not stop the campaign.

| Channel | Live-domain target | Qualified opportunity pool | Expected mechanism |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Product, startup, SaaS, and AI directories | 20 | 45 | Self-submit, claim, or refresh moderated listings |
| Existing resource pages and tool roundups | 15 | 70 | Request a relevant addition or refresh with a specific reason |
| Eyewear, optometry, hairstyle, and beauty publishers | 10 | 45 | Visual companion, methodology citation, or article resource |
| Founder launches and product communities | 5 | 15 | Durable public product/profile pages, not transient posts |
| Guest contributions, expert quotes, and original-data assets | 5 | 25 | Contributed article, quote, benchmark, or methodology reference |
| Partners, agencies, SDK/integration pages, and customer ecosystems | 5 | 20 | Partner page, use case, integration note, or resource mention |
| **Total** | **60** | **220** | |

The pool is larger than the goal because approval and editorial response are outside VisuTry's control. A 220-domain pool allows for rejection, non-response, paid-only routes, duplicate listings, and low-quality exclusions.

## 5. Linkable offers

Cold outreach should not ask for a link to a generic homepage. Each target receives one relevant offer.

### A. Free private detector

- no login;
- no credits;
- face photo and raw landmarks stay in the browser;
- landmark mesh and measured geometry make the result inspectable;
- useful next step for articles that explain face shape or glasses selection.

### B. Publisher visual companion

- one clean detector screenshot;
- one short annotated measurement graphic;
- one face-shape-to-frame comparison visual;
- permission to embed with attribution;
- no user photo unless it is a licensed or approved demonstration asset.

### C. Citation-ready methodology

- how on-device landmarks are used;
- what the tool measures and does not measure;
- why face shape is a shortlist rather than a physical-fit claim;
- limitations around hairline, pose, camera angle, and mixed face shapes.

### D. Eyewear decision resource

- seven face-shape reference pages;
- high-confusion comparison pages;
- face measurement guide;
- glasses recommendations connected to visual try-on.

### E. Original benchmark

After enough anonymous detector runs and consented validation samples exist, publish an aggregate benchmark covering completion rate, image-quality failure modes, and classifier agreement. Do not publish facial images or individual biometric data.

### Weeks 1-2: foundation and controlled placements

- Reconcile existing links with GSC.

### Weeks 3-4: resource pages and comparison refreshes

- Research pages ranking for face-shape guides, glasses-by-face-shape, face-shape detectors, and virtual try-on tools.
- Send page-specific outreach that identifies the exact section where the free tool is useful.
- Offer an embeddable visual or concise methodology note instead of a generic product pitch.
- Launch or refresh durable founder/product pages.

Exit target: 18 cumulative live domains, 70 cumulative outreach/submission actions.

### Weeks 5-8: editorial assets and contribution

- Publish the citation-ready methodology page.
- Publish one original comparison or benchmark asset.
- Pitch guest contributions and expert quotes to eyewear, optometry, beauty, and ecommerce publications.
- Approach agencies and SDK/integration ecosystems with a concrete implementation or shopper-decision use case.
- Run one follow-up after 7 days and a final value-add follow-up after 21 days; then stop.

Exit target: 40 cumulative live domains, 150 cumulative qualified actions.

### Weeks 9-12: close the gap

- Reallocate effort toward channels with the highest live-link conversion.
- Replace paid-only, spammy, or non-indexable targets in the pool.
- Refresh unanswered pitches with a new asset only when there is a specific fit.
- Verify all live placements again and repair broken or incorrect destinations.

Exit target: 60 verified unique referring domains.

## 7. Weekly operating cadence

Minimum weekly output:

| Work | Weekly floor |
| --- | ---: |
| New qualified domains researched | 20 |
| Free directory/product submissions | 5 |
| Personalized editorial/resource outreach | 15 |
| Follow-ups | 10 |
| New or improved linkable asset | 1 every two weeks |
| Live-link verification | Every Friday |

Stop rules:

- After 30 messages to one segment with less than 3% human reply, change the offer or segment.
- Do not submit to a paid route without separate approval and evidence of quality.
- Do not send the same body to unrelated sites.
- Do not continue after a recipient declines or opts out.
- Do not trade product access, payment, or gifts for an undisclosed followed link.

## 8. Funnel and reporting

The operating funnel is:

`Researched → Qualified → Contact route verified → Drafted → Approved to send/submit → Sent/Submitted → Replied → Accepted → Live → Reverified`

Primary KPI:

- verified unique referring domains: `0-60` campaign-created domains;

Supporting diagnostics:

- qualified opportunity pool size;
- submissions and personalized messages;
- human reply rate;
- acceptance-to-live rate;
- average days to live;
- link type and topical segment;
- referral sessions and Detector completions by source;
- links lost or changed.

The campaign does not count traffic-less activity as success, but a legitimate live referring domain counts before GSC discovers it. GSC discovery is tracked separately.
