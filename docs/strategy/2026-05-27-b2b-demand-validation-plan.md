# VisuTry B2B Demand Validation Plan (30 Days)

**Date**: 2026-05-27  
**Owner**: Founder-led  
**Scope**: Validate merchant demand before committing major B2B engineering work.

---

## 1. Goal

In the next 30 days, prove whether VisuTry can convert interest into credible B2B pipeline for eyewear commerce.

Primary outcome targets:

- 20-50 qualified outbound targets identified.
- 5-10 high-fit targets shortlisted for custom outreach.
- 3-5 meaningful business conversations.
- 1-3 merchants or agencies entering design-partner or pilot discussion.

This plan is intentionally non-engineering-first. It prioritizes market proof over platform buildout.

---

## 2. Why This Plan Exists

VisuTry has a functioning try-on pipeline and early paid validation, but current bottleneck is not technical feasibility. The bottleneck is distribution and merchant demand certainty.

At this stage, the highest-leverage move is to validate:

1. Who responds.
2. Which use case they value.
3. Whether they will pay for a scoped pilot.

Only after this should major engineering tracks (merchant API/productized widget/platform apps) accelerate.

---

## 3. ICP and Segmentation

### Priority ICP A: Shopify-native DTC eyewear/sunglasses brands

- Typical profile: 20-300 key SKUs, mobile-heavy traffic, no strong current VTO.
- Core pain: product-page hesitation and style uncertainty.
- Decision maker: founder, ecommerce lead, growth/merchandising manager.

### Priority ICP B: Small-to-mid online eyewear retailers

- Typical profile: already selling frames online, need conversion confidence layer.
- Core pain: weak PDP differentiation, uncertain add-to-cart confidence.
- Decision maker: ecommerce manager or owner.

### Priority ICP C: Boutique ecommerce agencies (eyewear/fashion)

- Typical profile: 5-50 team size, recurring storefront build/optimization services.
- Core pain: need high-impact conversion add-on for clients without enterprise tooling.
- Decision maker: founder, partner, technical lead.

Out of scope (for this 30-day cycle):

- Enterprise chains with procurement-heavy cycles.
- Prescription-first medical workflows requiring PD/EHR promises.
- Full white-label commitments.

---

## 4. Core Offer to Test

Working value proposition:

> Launch AI glasses try-on on top SKUs in days, not months, without 3D modeling.  
> Get measurable engagement/conversion signals before scaling catalog coverage.

Pilot framing:

- 30-day design-partner pilot.
- 5-20 top SKUs.
- 100-500 successful renders target band.
- Weekly summary + end-of-pilot readout.

---

## 5. Assets to Build (Week 1)

1. **B2B Landing Page Copy (one page)**
   - Audience: merchants/agencies only.
   - Sections: pain, solution, pilot scope, proof style, CTA.
   - CTA: request demo / request pilot call.

2. **Pilot One-Pager**
   - Scope, timeline, input requirements, what is measured, what is not promised.
   - Include privacy and accuracy disclaimers.

3. **Custom Demo Pack**
   - 5-10 tailored examples based on target stores/SKUs.
   - Keep to realistic product imagery and category fit.

4. **Outreach Templates**
   - Merchant cold intro.
   - Agency partnership intro.
   - Follow-up sequence (D+3, D+7).

---

## 6. Weekly Execution Rhythm

### Week 1 (Days 1-7): Setup and Positioning

- Finalize ICP list and target account list (20-50).
- Prepare landing copy, pilot one-pager, and demo examples.
- Set simple CRM columns: target, segment, first contact, status, next step.

### Week 2 (Days 8-14): First Outreach Wave

- Send first wave to 20-30 targets.
- Track response quality, objections, and meeting requests.
- Iterate messaging quickly based on first reactions.

### Week 3 (Days 15-21): Focused Follow-Up and Demo Calls

- Run follow-up sequence for non-responders.
- Hold discovery/demo calls with responders.
- Capture objections: performance, privacy, ROI proof, integration concerns.

### Week 4 (Days 22-30): Conversion and Decision

- Push best leads toward pilot discussion.
- Produce a 30-day review: conversion funnel, objections, segment quality.
- Decide go/no-go for next engineering tranche.

---

## 7. KPI Dashboard (Minimum)

Top-of-funnel:

- Number of qualified targets.
- First-message response rate.
- Positive reply rate.

Mid-funnel:

- Demo call booking rate.
- Discovery-to-pilot-discussion conversion.
- Segment-level conversion (A/B/C ICP).

Bottom signal:

- Number of design-partner candidates.
- Number of pilot-ready opportunities.

Quality signals:

- Common objections by frequency.
- Time-to-first-meaningful-reply.
- Message variant performance.

---

## 8. Go / No-Go Rules

Go (move to limited engineering acceleration) if:

- >= 3 meaningful conversations and >= 1 credible pilot-ready lead, or
- >= 2 agencies show active interest with named merchant opportunities.

No-Go (refine positioning before engineering) if:

- Response quality is weak despite 2 outreach iterations, and
- Objections indicate unclear value proposition rather than missing feature depth.

Pivot triggers:

- If agency response is consistently poor, shift to direct merchant focus.
- If direct merchant response is weak, narrow vertical further (e.g., sunglasses-only).
- If high interest but blocked by missing measurement, prioritize minimal attribution/events only.

---

## 9. Engineering Guardrail During Validation

Do **not** start heavy build tracks during this 30-day cycle:

- No full merchant platform refactor.
- No public Shopify app launch.
- No broad API productization.
- No deep workflow promises (PD/EHR/PMS integration).

Allowed minimal engineering only when directly needed for active lead conversion:

- Lightweight attribution fields.
- Basic event tracking needed for pilot reporting.
- Demo stability/performance fixes.

---

## 10. End-of-Cycle Outputs

By day 30, produce:

1. Pipeline snapshot (who responded, who advanced, who dropped).
2. Segment conclusion (which ICP pulls hardest).
3. Offer conclusion (which message converts best).
4. Objection map with mitigation strategy.
5. Recommendation for next 30-60 days:
   - Continue demand validation,
   - Start limited widget engineering,
   - Start Shopify private beta wrapper,
   - Or reframe target segment.
