## 1. Purpose

This document records external commercial references that help shape VisuTry's commercial strategy.

It is intentionally separate from `docs/strategy/commercial-strategy.md` so the strategy source of truth can stay concise. New market references, competitor observations, pricing notes, and workflow inspirations should be collected here first. Only durable conclusions should be promoted into the commercial strategy document.

Working rule:

> Benchmarks collect evidence. Commercial strategy makes decisions.

## 2. Benchmark Taxonomy

| Category | What it represents | Why it matters to VisuTry |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Optical practice workflow SaaS | Software that helps independent optical practices sell, pre-shop, recover, or manage frame interest. | Closest reference for VisuTry Store. |
| Eyewear VTO infrastructure | AR / virtual try-on / SDK / widget providers. | Useful for technical packaging, but not enough as a standalone commercial moat. |
| Eyewear brand or distributor sales enablement | Frame catalog and professional selling tools used by eyewear brands, reps, or distributors. | Useful reference for catalog-driven sales workflows. |

| Consumer free tools | Free face-shape, styling, or detection tools. | Useful for acquisition, SEO/GEO, and first-result UX. |

## 3. Primary Benchmarks

### 3.1 Optify

**Type:** Optical practice workflow SaaS / ecommerce enablement.

**Why it matters:** Optify is the strongest commercial benchmark for VisuTry's future shape. It is not merely a virtual try-on product. It combines practice website, frame catalog, ecommerce-style browsing, virtual try-on, favorites, pre-shop, walk-out recovery, conversion tracking, and analytics.

**Commercial lesson:**

> The valuable product is not image generation. The valuable product is an optical selling workflow that helps a practice convert patient or shopper intent.

**What VisuTry should learn:**

- Make merchant/practice workflow the product center.
- Connect catalog browsing, recommendation, try-on, favorites, lead capture, and follow-up.
- Treat analytics and recovery as business features, not secondary dashboards.
- Use VTO as one step inside a decision workflow.

**What VisuTry should not copy blindly:**

- Do not start by building every optical practice feature.
- Do not overbuild inventory sync before validating merchant demand.
- Do not assume independent practices are the only customer segment; eyewear sellers, stylists, and social sellers may be easier early adopters.

**Revenue note:** Publicly available information does not disclose exact annual revenue. Based on visible pricing and public customer wording, Optify appears more like a focused small vertical SaaS than a large enterprise SaaS. Any ARR estimate should remain a directional hypothesis, not a cited fact.

### 3.2 OGI / The Optical Foundry

**Type:** Eyewear brand portfolio and professional sales enablement reference.

**Why it matters:** OGI / The Optical Foundry is not primarily a SaaS product. It is closer to an eyewear brand / distributor portfolio using digital try-on and catalog tools to support optical professionals and customers.

**Commercial lesson:**

> Frame catalog + professional presentation + try-on can become a sales enablement system, even when software is not the main revenue product.

**What VisuTry should learn:**

- Frame catalog is a commercial asset, not just UI data.
- Professional users need a clean way to present, compare, and recommend frames.
- Try-on can support brand, distributor, reseller, and practice sales workflows.

**What VisuTry should not copy blindly:**

- OGI's revenue likely comes primarily from eyewear product sales, not software subscription.
- VisuTry should not model its revenue expectations directly on OGI unless it enters catalog ownership, distribution, or affiliate commerce.

### 3.3 Fittingbox / Ditto / Luna

**Type:** Eyewear virtual try-on infrastructure.

**Why it matters:** These companies represent the infrastructure side of eyewear VTO: frame digitization, AR rendering, SDK/API, retailer integrations, and large platform relationships.

**Commercial lesson:**

> VTO infrastructure can be valuable, but it is also a crowded and technically demanding layer. Basic try-on alone is not enough differentiation for VisuTry.

**What VisuTry should learn:**

- Widget / SDK packaging matters.
- Frame-image quality and rendering reliability matter.
- Merchants eventually care about integration, performance, and support.

**What VisuTry should not copy blindly:**

- Do not compete directly as a pure AR infrastructure vendor in the first phase.
- Do not make real-time 3D AR a prerequisite for commercial validation.
- Do not delay workflow validation while trying to match enterprise-grade VTO platforms.

### 3.4 Perfect Corp

**Type:** Enterprise beauty/fashion/eyewear AR commerce platform.

**Why it matters:** Perfect Corp shows how try-on, recommendation, and commerce enablement can be packaged for brands and retailers.

**Commercial lesson:**

> Recommendation + try-on + commerce integration is stronger than try-on alone.

**What VisuTry should learn:**

- Position around shopper confidence and decision support.
- Treat recommendations as part of the commercial product.
- Make the before/after business value visible.

**What VisuTry should not copy blindly:**

- Do not start with enterprise sales complexity.
- Do not overfit to large-brand requirements before proving small-merchant workflow.

### 3.5 Warby Parker / Zenni / GlassesUSA / EyeBuyDirect

**Type:** DTC eyewear retailers.

**Why they matter:** These companies show mature consumer journeys for choosing frames online: catalog browsing, recommendations, try-on, reviews, price comparison, and purchase flow.

**Commercial lesson:**

> Consumers need confidence before buying eyewear online. Face guidance, frame selection, virtual try-on, and comparison all reduce hesitation.

**What VisuTry should learn:**

- Consumers need guided decision flows, not only a generated image.
- Try-on should connect to frame shortlist and comparison.
- The strongest consumer CTA is often a decision step, not a subscription pitch.

**What VisuTry should not copy blindly:**

- These are retailers, not SaaS references.
- Their economics depend on selling eyewear products, not only software.
- VisuTry should not assume retailer-level catalog ownership in the early stage.

### 3.6 FaceCalculators / Detect Face Shape / FaceShape.io

**Type:** Consumer free tools and SEO/GEO acquisition references.

**Why they matter:** These products validate free face-shape detection as a strong consumer acquisition entry point.

**Commercial lesson:**

> A low-friction free tool can capture broad consumer demand, but the result must lead into a more valuable decision workflow.

**What VisuTry should learn:**

- Put the tool before long marketing copy.
- Reduce friction: no login, clear privacy promise, immediate result.
- Use face-shape result as a gateway to glasses advice, try-on, comparison, and content.

**What VisuTry should not copy blindly:**

- Do not overclaim accuracy before validation.
- Do not let the product end at a face-shape label.
- Do not treat ad monetization as the main early business model.

## 4. What VisuTry Should Learn Across Benchmarks

The strongest shared pattern is:

> catalog + recommendation + try-on + comparison + saved intent + follow-up.

For VisuTry, this suggests the future commercial product should look like an eyewear decision workspace rather than a single image-generation utility.

Recommended product direction:

1. Use free consumer tools to capture demand.
2. Use Glasses Advisor to create a personalized shortlist.
3. Use Virtual Try-On and Frame Compare for visual validation.
4. Use saved favorites, report links, and dashboard records to create professional or merchant workflows.
5. Add catalog, lead capture, analytics, and follow-up for B2B monetization.

---

## 5. What VisuTry Should Not Copy

VisuTry should avoid three traps:

1. **Pure VTO trap**  
   Competing only as another virtual try-on widget makes the product easy to compare against larger infrastructure providers.

2. **Retailer trap**  
   Copying Warby Parker or Zenni too directly would imply inventory ownership, fulfillment, returns, and retail economics that VisuTry does not currently have.

3. **SEO page-count trap**  
   Copying free tools or old programmatic SEO playbooks without a decision workflow risks traffic without commercial conversion.

---

## 6. Strategic Implications for VisuTry

The benchmark evidence supports the commercial strategy direction:

> VisuTry should evolve into a lightweight AI eyewear advisor and try-on workspace for small optical stores, eyewear sellers, stylists, and high-intent consumers.

This means:

- `VisuTry Free` captures consumer demand.
- `Glasses Advisor` creates recommendation depth.
- `Virtual Try-On` validates specific frames.
- `Frame Compare` supports final choice.
- `VisuTry Studio` packages the workflow for stylists and consultants.
- `VisuTry Store` packages the workflow for small merchants and optical sellers.
- `VisuTry Widget / SDK` becomes a distribution shell after workflow validation.

4. Which conversion metric matters most to merchants: lead submission, appointment request, saved frame, add-to-cart lift, or return visit?
5. Can VisuTry create a report format that both consumers and professional users value?
